Descriptive Statistics - Hour

Most bike crashes appear to occur from 1pm to 6pm, and from 7pm to 12am. It is interesting to see that the difference between ‘7am-12pm’ and ‘1pm-6pm’ is big, especially if we expect there would be more traffic at peak hours, both in the morning and after work hours.

However, it seems that there are other factors that contribute more to accidents than the number of cars on the street. If there are more cars, traffic gets to slow down, and may be why there are less crashes.

One plausible explanation is that it tends to get darker after work, and therefore, the visibility of traffic decreases. People could be physically and mentally more exhausted after work, and therefore more likely to get distracted. However, this hypothesis needs to be further studied by other measures.
